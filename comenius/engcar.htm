EUROPEAN HANDBOOK FOR TUTORS

 

1. Expectations and objectives

 

GENERAL

The project concerns work placements, typically of 4 -5 weeks duration, which will not necessarily count towards any exam qualifications. Longer qualifying placements, perhaps entitled: Work placements in partner countries: the exact nature of a qualifying placement, could be considered in a third European project i.e. Leonardo.

 

These 4 -week placements represent an intermediate phase before the"qualifying" Leonardo placement; should the required duration of 4 - 5 weeks prove an obstacle, a shorter placement could be considered.

The placement proper will be preceded by a settling-in period at the receiving educational establishment, led by a tutor from the receiving establishment who speaks the placement student’s mother tongue, or, should this not prove practicable, possibly by a student tutor / "buddy".

 

ELIGIBILTY

These placements will be open to students on vocational courses, preferably those who have completed an initial period of work placement, of at least 4 weeks’ duration, in their home country; their suitability for an overseas placement will be assessed on completion of this first placement period. The student will already have a basic background knowledge of the profession in question...and will demonstrate specific qualities determined and assessed by course tutors. The student should preferably have reached the age of consent, i.e. 18 years.

3. WORK PLACEMENTS: CONTENT AND REQUIREMENTS

If at all possible, the receiving business should have some kind of business or professional links with the placement trainee’s country of origin.

THE SENDING ESTABLISHMENT

The sending establishment will determine the work placement trainee’s professional and linguistic competence through records of work and evaluation of the previous placement period.

RECEIVING ESTABLISHMENT AND BUSINESS

The receiving establishment and business will determine a programme of work, with the aid of the observations of the sending establishment with regard to competence. This will necessitate the production of a document, which will form the basis of practical work required from the three establishments involved.

  

4. TEACHING STAFF

GENERAL

Contracts will have been agreed between the businesses concerned and the educational establishments, based on Leonardo guidelines. All 3 tutors and the trainee involved in a given placement should have a common working language.

 

THE ROLE OF THE TUTOR IN THE RECEIVING BUSINESS

(S)he will:-

  • negotiate the exact content of the placement with the tutor in the local (or receiving) college.
  • deal with the local tutor as a matter of course, and, should the need arise, the tutor in the sending establishment.
  • assess the pre-agreed competencies of the trainee
  • feed back to the local tutor on a regular basis with regard to the trainee’s progress, integration, etc..

 

THE ROLE OF THE LOCAL TUTOR (I.E. THE TUTOR IN THE RECEIVING COLLEGE)

(S)he will:-

  • determine,in conjunction with the tutor from the sending establishment, anything necessary to the trainee’s smooth integration, prior to the start of a placement.
  • act as an intermediary between the sending tutor in the receiving business.
  • keep the tutor from the sending establishment informed of the trainee’s progress on a regular basis.
  • determine the professional ‘tasks’ allocated to the trainee.
  • assess the trainee’s linguistic progress.
  • monitor the trainee’s integration and manner
  • visit the trainee at the workplace

THE ROLE OF THE TUTOR IN THE SENDING ESTABLISHMENT

  • (S)he will assess and thence inform the tutor in the receiving college of the trainee’s aptitude and ability to integrate into the chosen workplace.
  • In conjunction with other staff, (s)he will assess the trainee’s linguistic competence as it affects integration.
  • This subject tutor will work in conjunction with a language teacher; one will assess vocational competence, the other communication skills as they affect integration into the workplace.
  • (S)he will determine the expected outcomes of the placement.

THE STUDENT TUTOR / BUDDY

  • This could be a student attending the local (or receiving ) college and on placement in the same company as the overseas trainee.
  • Said student would accompany the trainee on a daily basis, in order to facilitate his or her integration.
  • Ideally, the student buddy would subsequently undertake a reciprocal period of work experience.
  • Ideally also, the buddy would accommodate the placement trainee.

     

    5. EVALUATION OF THE EXCHANGE

THE STUDENT TRAINEE

  • Daily log / diary (tasks undertaken)
  • Additional notes and thoughts (e.g. comments on the company, working practices, health and safety) in an appropriate format, such as a short report, photo diary, etc.
    •

 

THE TUTORS

Each educational establishment will submit a simple grid to the other college and the receiving business.

A common assessment procedure will be agreed on (Stage 3 of the Comenius project, 1998 -99), incorporating:-

  • the expectations / demands of the receiving business in terms of knowledge and behaviour
  • the trainee’s communication skills, manner and integration
  • weekly monitoring / evaluation visits (the final one of the which will be carried out by the tutor from the sending establishment accompanied by the aforementioned language teacher).
  • a final evaluation carried out jointly by the three tutors involved in the placement

 

 6. ADMINISTRATION

 

GENERAL POINTS TO CONSIDER

  • Contracts to be signed by all four parties involved (sending establishment, receiving establishment, business and trainee)
  • Insurance
  • Possible restrictions relating to working codes of practice (e.g. number of working hours for younger trainees, etc.)
  • Possible problems associated with accommodation will need to be addressed. Cost and nature of reception must be agreed upon before any contract is signed.
